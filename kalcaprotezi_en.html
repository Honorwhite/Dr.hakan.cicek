Hip Replacement Surgery in Adana

Hip replacement surgery in Adana is a procedure performed by Prof. Dr. Hakan Çiçek to replace damaged joint surfaces with an artificial joint. This treatment is typically recommended for patients suffering from severe hip arthritis (osteoarthritis), damage following a hip dislocation, fractures, or avascular necrosis. When pain limits daily activities, reduces walking distance, and non-surgical treatments like physical therapy or medication no longer provide relief, hip arthroplasty becomes the most effective solution.

Prof. Dr. Hakan Çiçek utilizes modern surgical techniques and high-quality prosthetic materials to enhance patients' quality of life. Modern hip replacement surgery is performed with tissue-friendly approaches, allowing for quick mobilization. The primary goal is a rapid reduction in pain and the restoration of independent movement.

What is a hip prosthesis, and who needs it? When the cartilage in the hip joint is irreversibly damaged, worn surfaces are replaced with a durable system made of metal and polyethylene components. This eliminates friction-related pain and restores range of motion.

Hip replacement treatment in Adana includes:

  • Detailed clinical examination and radiological assessment (X-ray, MRI).
  • Patient-specific prosthesis selection and surgical planning.
  • Early-stage mobilization (walking) post-surgery.
  • Personalized rehabilitation and follow-up process.
Most patients are able to stand and walk the day after surgery. While full recovery varies, returning to social life within a few weeks is typically achievable. The lifespan of a modern hip prosthesis can reach 20-25 years depending on the surgical technique and the patient's activity level.

If hip pain is disrupting your sleep, making stairs difficult, or if medications are no longer helping, hip replacement surgery might be the right option for you. Contact Prof. Dr. Hakan Çiçek in Adana to determine the best path for your hip health.

Hip Arthritis Symptoms

  • • Severe pain in the groin area
  • • Limping while walking
  • • Difficulty wearing socks or tying shoes
  • • Persistent hip pain even at rest

Benefits of Treatment

  • • Pain-free living
  • • Increased mobility
  • • Correction of leg length discrepancy
  • • Significant increase in quality of life

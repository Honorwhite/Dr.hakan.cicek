Knee Ligament Injuries and Modern Treatment Methods

Knee ligament injuries are a significant health problem, especially common among athletes and active individuals. Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL), posterior cruciate ligament (PCL), medial collateral ligament (MCL), and lateral collateral ligament (LCL) injuries significantly affect the stability and function of the knee joint. Thanks to modern orthopedic approaches and arthroscopic surgical techniques, high success rates are achieved in the treatment of these injuries.

Knee Ligament Injuries

  • • Anterior Cruciate Ligament (ACL) Tear
  • • Posterior Cruciate Ligament (PCL) Tear
  • • Medial Collateral Ligament (MCL) Tear
  • • Lateral Collateral Ligament (LCL) Tear
  • • Multiple Ligament Injuries

Treatment Methods

  • • Arthroscopic Ligament Repair
  • • Ligament Reconstruction
  • • Minimally Invasive Surgery
  • • Physical Therapy and Rehabilitation
  • • Personalized Treatment Planning

Symptoms of Knee Ligament Injuries

  • • Sudden and severe knee pain
  • • Swelling and bruising in the knee
  • • Loss of knee stability
  • • Difficulty walking and climbing stairs
  • • Popping sound and locking sensation in the knee
